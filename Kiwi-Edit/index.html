Kiwi-Edit: Versatile Video Editing
via Instruction and Reference Guidance

Yiqi Lin, Guoqiang Liang, Ziyun Zeng, Zechen Bai, Yanzhe Chen,
Mike Zheng Shou✉️

ShowLab, National University of Singapore   

We present KiwiEdit, a unified framework for high-quality video editing that seamlessly handles three editing paradigms: text instruction-guided editing, reference image-guided editing, and the novel reference image + text instruction joint editing. By unifying these tasks under a single model with shared spatiotemporal control, KiwiEdit achieves state-of-the-art results across all three settings while maintaining strong temporal consistency.

Method

KiwiEdit is built on a video diffusion backbone and introduces a unified conditioning framework that jointly handles text instructions, reference images, and their combination. Our approach rests on three pillars:

  • Unified Multi-Modal Conditioning: We design a shared encoder that processes text instructions and reference images through a cross-attention mechanism, enabling seamless switching between and combination of conditioning modalities.
  • Spatiotemporal Mask Control: A unified mask-based control mechanism allows spatial precision (editing specific regions) and temporal control (editing specific frames), supporting both local and global edits across all three paradigms.
  • Scalable Data Pipeline: We construct a large-scale training dataset covering all three editing paradigms through a combination of synthetic generation, automated captioning, and reference-instruction pair mining.

Overview of KiwiEdit. Given an input video, KiwiEdit processes text instructions through an instruction encoder and reference images through a visual encoder. Both conditioning signals are fused via cross-attention and fed into the video diffusion backbone along with spatiotemporal mask control signals. The model supports three editing modes: instruction-only, reference-only, and joint instruction+reference editing.

Unified Conditioning

Rather than training separate models for instruction-guided and reference-guided editing, KiwiEdit uses a shared cross-attention conditioning mechanism. Text instructions are encoded via a language model and projected into the conditioning space, while reference images are encoded via a visual encoder (e.g., CLIP or DINOv2) and projected into the same space. During joint editing, both signals are concatenated along the sequence dimension before cross-attention, allowing the model to attend to both modalities simultaneously.

Training Strategy

We train KiwiEdit in three stages: (1) instruction-only editing on large-scale synthetic instruction pairs, (2) reference-only editing on style and appearance transfer data, and (3) joint fine-tuning on curated reference+instruction pairs with mixed-modality dropout. The mixed-modality dropout randomly drops one conditioning signal during training, ensuring the model learns to gracefully handle all three paradigms.
